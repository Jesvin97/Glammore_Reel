# Quick Production Reference: On-Set Reel Filming

**Print this. Bring to every shoot. Reference while filming.**

---

## PRE-SHOOT CHECKLIST (Day Before)

- [ ] Script reviewed + memorized (voiceover)
- [ ] Props/products gathered (haircare products, cosmetics, tools)
- [ ] Lighting set up (ring light, natural window, golden hour timing)
- [ ] Client confirmed + hair/makeup styled appropriately
- [ ] Phone/camera charged (100%)
- [ ] External mic tested (audio clarity critical)
- [ ] Tripod/stand positioned
- [ ] Backup phone storage (delete old videos to free space)
- [ ] Backup client on standby (if primary cancels)

---

## REEL STRUCTURE (Every Reel Follows This)

```
[0-3 sec] HOOK
↓
[3-30 sec] BODY (proof, education, or transformation)
↓
[30-50 sec] CTA (explicit call to action)
↓
[50-60 sec] OUTRO (your salon name, WhatsApp, Google)
```

**Golden rule:** Hook in first 3 seconds or people swipe. Make the first visual ARRESTING.

---

## SHOT TYPES NEEDED FOR EACH REEL

### BEFORE/AFTER REELS (1, 3, 9, 14, 18, 25, 30)

**Before footage:**
- Wide: full person, good lighting
- Close-up: damage/issue clearly visible (frizz, breakage, color fade)
- Use same angle/lighting for before & after (comparison integrity)

**Process footage:**
- Hands close-up: detail work (cutting, coloring, applying product)
- Wide: full view of stylist + client
- Multiple angles: product application, tool work, technique detail

**After footage:**
- Immediate after: minute 0 (day 1 result)
- Follow-up after: week 4, week 8, week 12 (progression proof)
- Same angles as before (consistency)
- Client reaction: genuine emotion, 10–15 sec uninterrupted

**Ratio:** 10 sec before : 30 sec process : 20 sec after

---

### EDUCATION REELS (5, 10, 11, 13, 15, 16, 20, 26)

**Intro:** Title card or expert speaking (5 sec)

**Three key points:** Each point gets:
- Text overlay (1 line, max 10 words)
- Visual demo or example (10 sec each)
- Expert explaining (5 sec talking head, optional)

**Recap:** Summarize the 3 points (5 sec)

**CTA:** Explicit ask (5 sec)

**Ratio:** 5 intro : 30 body (3 points × 10 sec) : 10 recap : 10 CTA

---

### PROOF/TESTIMONIAL REELS (2, 6, 12, 17, 27)

**Client intro:** Name, service, "Why did you come?" (5 sec)

**Service footage:** Timelapse or key moments (30 sec)

**Client reaction:** Genuine, unscripted, full emotion (15 sec minimum)

**Quick facts:** What changed, cost, timeline (5 sec text overlay)

**Testimonial:** Client speaking (15–20 sec, one question)

**Ratio:** 5 intro : 30 service : 15 reaction : 5 facts : 15 testimonial

---

### MYTH-BUSTING REELS (8, 21, 23)

**Hook:** The wrong belief stated bluntly (3 sec)

**The myth broken down:** Why people believe it (5 sec)

**Reality explained:** What actually happens (10 sec)

**Proof:** Visual demonstration or case study (20 sec)

**The better way:** Your solution (10 sec)

**CTA:** Explicit ask (5 sec)

**Ratio:** 3 hook : 5 myth : 10 reality : 20 proof : 10 solution : 5 CTA

---

## AUDIO RECORDING (VOICEOVER)

**Record separately, not on-set:** Find quiet room, use external mic.

**Script delivery:**
- Conversational, not robotic
- Pace: Slightly slower than normal (leaves room for text overlays)
- Enthusiasm: Match the reel's vibe (myth-busting = confident; proof = excited)
- Pauses: Pause 1 sec between each logical thought (easier to edit)
- Multiple takes: Record each sentence 2–3 times (pick the best)

**Editing:** Drop voiceover on timeline, sync with visuals in CapCut

---

## LIGHTING CHECKLIST

**Best:** Golden hour (1 hour before sunset) or morning (8–10am)

**Acceptable:** Overcast day (soft light, no harsh shadows)

**Avoid:** Midday harsh sun (shadows under eyes, unflattering)

**Indoors:** Position client facing large window, use ring light for fill

**Close-ups (makeup, hair detail):** Ring light mandatory (even lighting)

**Group shots (bridal team, staff): Natural window light + reflector for fill**

---

## PHONE CAMERA SETTINGS

**Video:**
- Resolution: 1080p 30fps (or 4K 30fps if storage available)
- Format: Portrait (9:16 ratio for Instagram)
- Stabilization: ON

**Audio:**
- Record with external mic (built-in phone audio = poor)
- Test levels before recording (should show -12 to -6dB peaks)

**Focus:**
- Tap the subject before recording (lock focus)
- Relock every new shot (focus creep ruins footage)

## EDITING WORKFLOW (CapCut, ~2 hours per reel)

1. **Import footage:** Organize by scene (before, during, after)
2. **Arrange timeline:** Drag clips in sequence, rough timing
3. **Trim:** Cut out blinks, mistakes, pauses
4. **Add voiceover:** Sync audio to key visuals
5. **Text overlays:** Add key points (3–4 per reel, timing 2 sec each)
6. **Transitions:** Cuts only (no dissolves, no spins—professional)
7. **Music:** Add subtle background track (royalty-free, matching vibe)
8. **Color grade:** Slight brightness boost (match before/after lighting)
9. **Final review:** Watch full reel, check pacing and messaging
10. **Export:** 1080p 30fps, H.264 codec

## RESPONSE PROTOCOL

**Comment posted on your reel:**
- Respond within 1 hour (algorithm boost)
- Thank them + answer their question
- Ask a follow-up (keeps conversation going)

**DM asking about booking:**
- Respond within 2 hours (max)
- Confirm interest + send WhatsApp link or appointment link
- If they book, confirm date/time + remind them of location/parking

**Negative comment:**
- Respond professionally, never defensive
- "Thanks for the feedback. DM us and we'll help."
- Don't delete; engagement (even negative) helps reach

## POSTING WORKFLOW

**Day of posting (2 hours before 6pm):**

1. Export final reel from CapCut (mp4, 1080p)
2. Upload to Instagram (Reels > Create > Select video)
3. Write caption (copy from calendar)
4. Add link (WhatsApp URL or Google booking link in first comment)
5. Select cover frame (thumbnail) — choose the most arresting moment
6. Schedule post for 6:00 PM IST (exactly)
7. Set reminder to monitor comments in first hour (respond to all)

## COMMON MISTAKES (Avoid These)

❌ **Lighting too dark:** Footage looks amateur, hard to see details
→ **Fix:** Always use ring light + position client facing window

❌ **Audio too quiet:** Voiceover barely audible
→ **Fix:** Test mic levels before recording; record closer to mic

❌ **Too many transitions:** Looks cheap, distracts from content
→ **Fix:** Use cuts only (no dissolves, spins, zooms)

❌ **CTA unclear:** "DM us" instead of "DM 'keratin'"
→ **Fix:** Give specific keyword to DM (easier for viewer, filters messages)

❌ **No client permission:** Client in reel without consent
→ **Fix:** Get written permission + photo release before filming

❌ **Text overlays too small:** Unreadable on mobile
→ **Fix:** Use CapCut text at 50+ size; test on phone before posting

❌ **Before/after inconsistent angles:** Unfair comparison, looks fake
→ **Fix:** Use same lighting, angle, and distance for before & after

## TROUBLESHOOTING

**"Video looks blurry"**
→ Check phone focus locked; wipe lens; increase lighting

**"Audio has background noise"**
→ Record in quieter room; use external mic; denoiser in CapCut

**"Pacing feels slow"**
→ Trim silence; cut out blinks/mistakes; speed up timelapse by 2x

**"Lighting inconsistent between shots"**
→ Adjust ring light before each new shot; bracket exposures (film darker + lighter)

**"Client looks uncomfortable"**
→ Chat with them before filming; explain what you're doing; pause if they seem stressed

**"Can't get good before/after photos"**
→ Shoot before-photo on phone, not as reel footage; simplifies editing
